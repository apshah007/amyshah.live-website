More than 10 Ways to Increase Creativity

By Amy Shah

Problem: Forced Social Isolation Causes Neural Cravings Similar to Hunger:

New research highlights the profound effect of severe social isolation on the brain

https://blogs.scientificamerican.com/beautiful-minds/forced-social-isolation-causes-neural-craving-similar-to-hunger/

Solutions!

  1. Take an in person or online class about something that is compelling for you. https://ccsuconed.wordpress.com/2017/01/23/the-top-7-benefits-of-learning-a-new-skill/

  1. Make something new with your hands. It could be cooking, woodworking, crafts or anything else! Use your tactile senses to choose materials to work with. Use mindfulness as you complete the task. Make a collage. https://www.insidehighered.com/blogs/gradhacker/make-things-your-hands

  1. Journal regularly about experiences to meaningfully feel them. Take time to remember your days, specific events, or memories with people you cherish. Come back to these memories in your journal later. https://www.forbes.com/sites/bryancollinseurope/2019/11/05/the-surprising-creative-benefits-of-journal-writing/?sh=65998eb6110a

  1. Read periodicals or the newspaper to find new ideas. Learn about new teas, new types of shoes, new artificial intelligence machines, or even vacation resorts and musicians!

https://www.mitchellsny.com/blog/why-it-is-important-to-read-the-newspaper/

  1. Repurpose something you would ordinarily throw away. What can you do with orange peels?  Make orange zest to add to your favorite dishes.  What can you do with used tea bags? Save them for a compost pile in your backyard.

https://www.diyncrafts.com/6081/repurpose/100-ways-repurpose-reuse-broken-household-items

  1. Exercise.  Find something that excites you to do. Follow your intuition, but make sure you enjoy it. The mental benefits of positive exercise are as much as the physical benefits. Better and less negative ideas come when you are feeling well. Being healthy helps with creativity!

https://www.businessinsider.com/exercise-benefits-brain-creativity-stress-2016-11#:~:text=In%20humans%2C%20exercise%20increases%20the,also%20depends%20on%20this%20region

  1. Try new scents and flavors in your food and environment. Experts say that your concentration can be improved with lemon and peppermint for example.

https://www.inc.com/kate-l-harrison/scent-of-success-studies-show-aromatherapy-can-boost-your-memory-focus.html

 

  1. Listen to new or “new to you” music - even something you ordinarily would not like.  https://www.ucf.edu/pegasus/your-brain-on-music/

  1. Read books. https://worldliteracyfoundation.org/reading-enhances-imagination/

  1. Try something that is not natural for you. It could be learning a new dance style, learning a new language, even learning about different philosophies of thought or religion. https://blog.gardenuity.com/trying-new-things/

  1. Try going to a new place. You can even go to a place you have been before, but try to experience it differently.  That could mean going with a different person or going at a different time.

https://www.vogue.com/article/can-travel-make-you-more-creative#:~:text=New%20research%20suggests%20travel%20can,more%20inspired%20version%20of%20you

  1. Turn off your phone and other electronic devices.

https://www.boundless.org/adulthood/your-phone-may-be-killing-your-creativity/
